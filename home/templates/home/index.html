The Four Pillars

Bharatnatayam derives its name from its four foundational components

BHA-VA

Expression and emotion. The dancer channels deep sentiments to narrate spiritual and mythic tales.

RA-GA

Melodic frameworks. The classical Carnatic music notes that accompany and guide the dancer's feet.

TA-LA

Rhythm and meter. The strict mathematical beat intervals that govern the complexity of footwork.

NA-TYA

The sacred union of drama and pure physical dance, transforming the performance into sacred theater.

Sacred Mudras

Master the language of hand gestures (Hastas)

Pataka

Mudra #1

Gesture: Flat open palm with all fingers held straight and close together.

Used to signify a forest, a cloud, the sky, shielding, or welcoming guests.

Tripataka

Mudra #2

Gesture: Similar to Pataka, but with the ring finger bent downwards.

Represents a crown, a tree branch, a flame, a thunderbolt, or a bird.

Alapadma

Mudra #3

Gesture: All fingers separated and beautifully curled outward like a lotus.

Representing a fully bloomed lotus flower, beauty, a mirror, or the moon.
